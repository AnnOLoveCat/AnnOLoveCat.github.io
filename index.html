About Me

Hello! I’m Annu(Gordon), a graduate from the Department of Information Management, National Taipei University of Business (NTUB).

I’m passionate about the world of technology — from artificial intelligence and machine learning to software development and data analysis. I believe that in this fast-changing digital era, mastering information systems is the key to staying competitive.

I am currently focusing on the field of AI detection, shifting my interest from traditional algorithm research to more practical and impactful applications of artificial intelligence. I’ve been exploring object detection technologies such as Ultralytics and the YOLO family, and I have a foundational understanding of how AI model training works — including data preprocessing, image augmentation (like intentional blur or distortion to enhance robustness), and generating training labels through tools like LabelImg, which the model later uses for self-correction and iterative improvement.

My present goals are to enhance model accuracy, speed up training efficiency, and deepen my understanding of how techniques like Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) can support intelligent systems. I aim to strengthen my skills in building reliable, real-world AI detection solutions and move closer to becoming a professional capable of applying innovative AI technologies to solve practical problems.
